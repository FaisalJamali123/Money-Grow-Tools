    Frequently Asked Questions

    Everything you need to know about 1099 taxes

    How much tax do I owe on 1099 income?

    +
    1099 contractors pay 15.3% self-employment tax plus 10-37% federal income tax plus 0-13.3% state income tax depending on your location. Total can range from 25-65% of your income. Use our calculator to see your exact amount.

    Do I need to pay quarterly taxes on 1099 income?

    +
    Yes, if you expect to owe $1,000 or more in taxes for the year. The IRS requires quarterly estimated tax payments due April 15, June 15, September 15, and January 15. Missing these deadlines results in penalties of 0.5% per month.

    What is the self-employment tax rate for 2025?

    +
    The self-employment tax rate is 15.3% total: 12.4% for Social Security (on first $168,600 of income) and 2.9% for Medicare (unlimited). High earners ($200k+ single, $250k+ married) pay an additional 0.9% Medicare surtax.

    Can I deduct business expenses from 1099 income?

    +
    Yes! Common deductions include: home office, vehicle mileage ($0.67/mile for 2025), software subscriptions, phone and internet (business portion), health insurance premiums, and retirement contributions (SEP-IRA, Solo 401k). Enter your deductions in our calculator to see tax savings.

    Which states have no income tax for 1099 workers?

    +
    Nine states have no income tax: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, Wyoming, and New Hampshire. If you live in these states, you only pay federal and self-employment tax (no state tax added).

    What happens if I don't pay quarterly taxes?

    +
    The IRS charges a 0.5% penalty per month on unpaid taxes (6% annually), plus interest at the federal short-term rate +3%. Example: If you owe $10,000 and miss quarterly payments for a year, you'll pay $600-$1,100+ in penalties on top of the tax.
