Can I Write Off Groceries as a 1099 Worker?

The surprising answer: food bloggers, chefs & nutritionists can, but most freelancers can't. Use the interactive decision tree below to see if your profession qualifies—and how to document it correctly.

TL;DR - Quick Answer

  • Yes if groceries are an ordinary & necessary expense for your specific business (recipe development, client demos, content creation).
  • No if you're buying food for personal meals, family, or general “working from home.”
  • Documentation required: receipts + business-purpose log + published content link.

Required Documentation (IRS-Proof Checklist)

If you qualify, save all of the following for every grocery purchase:

1. Receipts

  • ✅ Itemized (shows individual food items)
  • ✅ Date & time visible
  • ✅ Store name clear
  • ❌ Credit-card slip alone (not enough)

2. Business-Purpose Log

  • ✅ Date & amount
  • ✅ Recipe name / client name
  • ✅ URL of published content (blog, YouTube, Instagram)
  • ✅ % of groceries actually used vs. discarded

FAQs

Can I deduct groceries if I work from home and eat while working?

No. The IRS considers food consumed while working a personal expense, even if you’re at your desk 12 hours a day.

What if I cook dinner and also photograph it for my blog?

Allocate the cost. If 30 % of the groceries are used for staged photos and 70 % eaten personally, only deduct 30 %.

Are condiments and spices deductible?

Yes, if purchased specifically for recipe testing or client demos and documented accordingly.

Can I use the standard meal allowance instead?

No. The per-diem meal allowance applies only to overnight business travel, not groceries at home.
