How 1099 Taxes Work for Freelancers

When you receive income as a 1099 contractor, taxes are not withheld. You are responsible for self-employment tax (Social Security and Medicare), federal income tax, and possibly state income tax. Understanding deductions and quarterly payments can significantly reduce what you owe. This calculator helps you plan accurately and confidently.

1099 Tax Calculator FAQs

Do I have to pay quarterly taxes as a 1099 worker?

Yes. If you expect to owe $1,000 or more in total taxes, the IRS generally requires quarterly estimated tax payments.

What expenses are deductible for freelancers?

Common deductions include home office expenses, internet, phone, mileage, equipment, software, and marketing costs.

What happens if I miss a quarterly payment?

Missing payments can result in IRS penalties and daily interest until the balance is paid.
